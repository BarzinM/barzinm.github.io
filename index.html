BIO

I like to make stuff. It started with making mechanisms with my LEGOs and failed attempts of fixing our clocks and vacuum cleaner(still don’t know what is going on inside clocks). I was lucky enough to do my middle school and high school in a branch of development of exceptional talents organization. From there, I got into a nice college to study aerospace engineering for my bachelor’s degree and learned a lot about design of multidisciplinary solutions. In that period, I got the chance to manage a team for design of a mid-sized regional aircraft and get a good experience on different levels of design. After that, I attended Michigan Tech to get my Master’s degree. I joined NASLab (Nonlinear and Autonomous Systems Laboratory) during my master's study and started working on autonomous vehicles (ground, underwater, aerial). Our team has been doing some interesting projects, two of which got a good amount of publicity. One was a solution for autonomous power distribution microgrids establishment and the other one was low-cost underwater glider development.
